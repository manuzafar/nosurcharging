# Product Vision
## nosurcharging.com.au

---

## The mission

Enable merchants everywhere to maximise their payment outcomes by driving radical transparency across the payments value chain — so businesses make better decisions, customers experience fairer pricing, and the payments ecosystem works better for everyone.

---

## The problem is global

Payment opacity is not an Australian problem. It is a structural feature of how the global payments industry has been built. Every market in the world shares the same fundamental condition: a payments value chain where each layer — card networks, issuers, acquirers, PSPs — extracts value in ways that are deliberately difficult for merchants to see, compare, or challenge.

Merchants sit at the bottom of this chain with the least information and the least negotiating power. They receive a single number — a merchant service fee — that hides everything that determines it.

---

## Australia is the trigger, not the destination

The RBA's March 2026 retail payments reform created a moment of acute merchant awareness in Australia. A surcharge ban, interchange caps, and mandatory transparency reporting — arriving simultaneously with a clear deadline of 1 October 2026.

That moment is the entry point. The platform is global.

nosurcharging.com.au is the Australian instance of a global merchant payments intelligence product. Every architectural decision is made with the global problem in mind, even as Phase 1 solves an urgent Australian problem.

---

## Geographic expansion

**Phase 1 — Australia (2026)**
Establish the product, prove the model, build the dataset.

**Phase 2 — New Zealand and United Kingdom (2027)**
English language markets with active payments regulation and existing Paid It credibility.

**Phase 3 — European Union and North America (2028+)**
Larger, more complex markets with deeper regulatory localisation needed.

The architecture supports this from day one. Country constants are modular. The assessment framework is universal. Launching in a new market is a configuration exercise, not a rebuild.

## Why independence matters

nosurcharging.com.au has no commercial relationship with any PSP, acquirer, or card scheme. This is not a positioning statement — it is an architectural constraint. No affiliate tracking. No PSP SDK integrations. No data sharing with payment providers. The platform is on the merchant's side, structurally and unconditionally.
