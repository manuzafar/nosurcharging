# RBA Reform — Reference Document

**Source:** RBA Conclusions Paper, 31 March 2026
**URL:** https://www.rba.gov.au/payments-and-infrastructure/review-of-retail-payments-regulation/2026-03/

## Key reform dates

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| 1 October 2026 | Surcharge ban effective (Visa, Mastercard, eftpos) |
| 1 October 2026 | Consumer credit IC cap → 0.30% |
| 1 October 2026 | Debit IC cap → 8c or 0.16% (lower of) |
| 30 October 2026 | Large acquirers publish average MSF data |
| 30 January 2027 | Pass-through report published |
| 1 April 2027 | Foreign card IC cap → 1.0% |
| Mid-2026 | RBA review: Amex, BNPL, PayPal, mobile wallets |

## Designated networks (ban applies)
Visa, Mastercard, eftpos

## NOT covered by the ban (can still surcharge)
Amex, BNPL (Afterpay, Zip, Klarna), PayPal — subject to mid-2026 review

## The surcharge ban mechanism (critical detail)
The RBA does not directly ban surcharges. It lifts the no-surcharge prohibition, allowing Visa and Mastercard to enforce their own contractual no-surcharge rules with acquirers. The ban is scheme-enforced through PSP contracts. Effective result: merchants cannot surcharge designated networks from 1 October 2026.

## Current interchange rates
- Debit: 9 cents per transaction
- Consumer credit: 0.52% of transaction value
- Commercial credit: 0.80%
- Foreign-issued cards: 2.80%

## From 1 October 2026
- Debit: 8 cents (or 0.16% — whichever is lower)
- Consumer credit: 0.30%
- Commercial: 0.80% (unchanged)
- Foreign: unchanged until April 2027

## From 1 April 2027
- Foreign IC cap: 1.0% — interchange only
- Scheme fees (~1.58%) are NOT capped
- True cost floor for foreign cards: approximately 2.58%–2.83%
- NEVER state the cap is 1.0% total — it is not

## Scheme fees (unregulated — unchanged by reform)
- Domestic: ~10.5bps (0.105%)
- Cross-border: ~158bps (1.58%)
- May rise post-reform — no cap exists

## Market context (source RBA)
- $1.8 billion total surcharges collected annually
- $1.6 billion paid by consumers
- 16% of Australian merchants currently surcharge
- $910 million projected annual IC saving across the market
- 90% of merchants did not switch PSP in 2024/25
