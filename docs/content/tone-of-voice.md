# Tone of Voice

Speak like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a payments expert. Not a consultant. Not a government resource.

## The primary audience

The café owner in Parramatta processing $800K in cards annually. They know they pay "something like 1.4%" to their bank. They do not know what interchange is. The tool serves them without making them feel ignorant.

## Plain English first

Rewrite any sentence containing: interchange, IC++, blended rate, basis points, MSF, acquirer, designated network. Translate to plain English equivalents.

## Specific over vague

- "$1,780 saving" not "costs may change"
- "Call Stripe and say..." not "contact your payment provider"
- "Three things to do this week" not "there are several considerations"
- "One email on 30 October" not "we'll keep you updated"

## Honest about uncertainty

The confidence badge is prominent. The assumptions panel is accessible. When we use RBA averages, we say so. When the number is an estimate, we say so.

## PSP name inline everywhere

In the action list, the PSP name appears literally:
- "Call Stripe and say..." — not "call your PSP"
- "Your ANZ statement will show..." — not "your statement"

## Action list format

Every action: [Time anchor] → [Specific action with exact words]

Not: "Consider renegotiating your merchant service fee."
Yes: "This week → Call Stripe and say: 'Interchange is falling on 1 October as part of the RBA reform. Can you commit to passing that saving through to my rate — in writing? What will my new rate be from October?'"

## Never use
- Revolutionary, game-changing, transformative
- Leverage, synergy, ecosystem (in merchant-facing copy)
- Passive voice in action items
- "We" when "I" (Manu) is more honest
